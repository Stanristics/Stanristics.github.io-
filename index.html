Research Projects

Project — 01 / 2024

Automated Data Collection of Rebel Weapon Endowments

w/ Harihara Viaish Ramaiah Sakthivel · LMU Munich, Political Science Dept.

A consulting system leveraging LLMs to automate the extraction and structuring of complex weapons data from vast, unstructured sources — conflict reports, news articles, field dispatches. Handles mixed data types while performing deep contextual reasoning to distinguish implicit details like "captured" vs. "purchased" weapons across multilingual corpora.

LLMs Python NLP Pipelines Data Engineering Political Science Conflict Research
  • Mixed data extraction: numeric (weapon counts), ordinal, and string (supplier names)
  • Contextual reasoning across "captured" vs. "purchased" weapon semantics
  • Multilingual document processing at terabyte scale
  • Presented at Ludwig Maximilian University Munich
Project — 02 / 2024

AHAMEFULA

Seminar Research · Computational Linguistics

A seminar paper analyzing the capability of large language models to understand the underlying structural patterns of low-context languages, using Igbo as a primary case study. Explores how BERT and similar architectures can bridge representation gaps for linguistically rich but underrepresented languages — with implications for cultural preservation and multilingual AI equity.

BERT Python NLP Linguistic Analysis Igbo Language Language Preservation
  • Analyzed LLM capability gaps in low-context, underrepresented languages
  • Case study: Igbo — a rich, explicitly structured African language
  • Applications in cultural heritage and multilingual AI fairness
  • Presented to academic audience; co-authored with research team

I'm a Graduate Statistician and Data Scientist with hands-on experience across financial services, logistics, and academic research. My foundation is in machine learning, data analysis, and predictive modelling — and I'm passionate about using data to solve problems that actually matter.
